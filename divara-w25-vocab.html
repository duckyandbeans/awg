✨ The Magic of Word Formation ✨

Words are like transformers! A single "root" word can change its shape depending on the job it needs to do in a sentence. We do this by snapping on special "upgrade parts" called Prefixes (at the beginning) and Suffixes (at the end).

1. The Noun (Thing/Person)

Names what it is. We often create nouns by adding suffixes like -er, -or, -ion, or -ing to a root word.

"He is a builder." (Person)
"That is a tall building." (Thing)

2. The Verb (Action)

Shows the action. This is usually the basic root word, but sometimes we add prefixes like re- (to do again) or un- (to undo).

"They build houses."
"They will rebuild it."

3. The Adjective (Describer)

Describes a noun. We usually build these by adding suffixes like -y, -ful, -ic, or -able.

"The water is icy." (ice + y)
"It is doubtful." (doubt + ful)

4. The Adverb (How it's done)

Describes an action. This is the easiest one to spot! We almost always build it by adding the suffix -ly.

"She walked quietly." (quiet + ly)
"He spoke widely." (wide + ly)
