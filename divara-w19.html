Today we are going to learn the difference between WILL and BE GOING TO.

Grammar Rules

will and be going to

will

Form: will + infinitive without the particle to

Affirmative Negative Question & Short Answer
I / you / he / she / it / we / they
will ('ll) work tomorrow.		 I / you / he / she / it / we / they
will not (won't) work tomorrow.		 Will I / you / he / she / it / we / they work tomorrow?

Yes, ... will.
No, ... won't.

Used to describe:

  • Future actions and events: The hotel will have ten employees.
  • Predictions: It will be a great job! You will enjoy working there.
  • Requests and offers: Will you help me? Of course I will!
  • Spontaneous decisions: Okay. I'll ask Mike to help me.

⚠️ Watch out!

To express a polite offer with the pronouns I and we, the question form Shall I (we) ...? is used.
Shall I tidy the office?

💡 Helpful hints

The form of the verb will remains unchanged for all pronouns:

  • Will you work in London? Yes, I will.
  • Tom won't work in London. He'll work in Vladivostok.

be going to

Form: be going to + infinitive

Affirmative Negative Question & Short Answer
I am ('m) going to work. I am not ('m not) going to work. Am I going to work?
Yes, I am. / No, I'm not.
He / she / it is ('s) going to work. He / she / it is not (isn't) going to work. Is he / she / it going to work?
Yes, he is. / No, he isn't.
You / we / they are ('re) going to work. You / we / they are not (aren't) going to work. Are you / we / they going to work?
Yes, we are. / No, we aren't.

Used to describe:

  • Intentions and plans: I am going to go to university when I leave school.
  • Future actions with evident signs: Greta is still sleeping. She's going to be late for work.
  • Future actions or events: The hotel is going to have ten employees.

⚠️ Watch out!

Quite often, sentences with will and be going to do not have significant differences:

  • The hotel will have thirty employees. They're going to be well-trained.
  • The hotel is going to have thirty employees. They'll be well-trained.
